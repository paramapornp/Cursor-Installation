Table 1: Brand Context, the command center
This is the most important table in the whole system.

Identity

Authority: [WHY ANYONE SHOULD LISTEN, e.g. systems built, clients helped, pipeline generated, posts written, workflows shipped, proof across the market]

Make the brand synonymous with practical authority in its niche, not generic AI hype

We are NOT optimizing for likes, follows, or virality.

A few things about this table:
The Identity section is not filler.

The AI uses this to calibrate every word.

"Helping businesses automate work" produces completely different content than "helping commercial teams build AI sales, marketing, CRM and reporting infrastructure around the tools they already use."

Be specific, painfully specific.

The "we are NOT optimizing for likes" line is a forcing function.

It stops the AI from drifting toward viral content patterns and keeps it focused on the actual goal, which is pipeline.

Spend the most time on this table.

It sets the standard for everything else.

Hooks, the most important engine part
Hooks decide 70%+ of LinkedIn post performance.

You can have the best post in the world, but if line 1 doesn't stop them, nobody reads it.

These are hooks that perform on LinkedIn specifically.

Twitter hooks don't work here, Instagram hooks don't work here.

LinkedIn has its own pattern.

Rule: First line must be visible BEFORE the "see more" cutoff.

LinkedIn truncates at around 210 characters.

The Authority Result Hook
"I've helped [N] [type of clients] [achieve outcome]. Here's the [N] rules I follow:"

Examples:

"I've helped teams build outbound systems that touch 12,000+ prospects a month. Here's what actually matters:"

"I've built AI automation systems across sales, content, CRM and reporting. Here's where most companies waste money:"

"I've worked across global marketing, AI automation and GTM systems. Here's the pattern I keep seeing:"

Best for: middle of funnel, building authority

The Specific Number Hook
"[Specific outcome] in [specific timeframe]. Here's what actually moved the needle:"

Examples:

"2,000 personalised outbound messages across email and LinkedIn. Here's what actually mattered:"

"30 prospects surfaced for one client from a system that replaced manual website crawling. Here's the structure:"

"10,000 leads routed through one intake system sounds simple until the agencies, forms and CRM rules get involved."

Best for: bottom of funnel, case studies

The Contrarian Hook
"You don't need [conventional advice]. You need [actual answer]."

Examples:

"You don't need more AI tools. You need the tools connected properly."

"You don't need another SDR. You need your follow-up process fixed."

"You don't need to rip out your stack. You need an operating layer around it."

"You don't need more content. You need content that warms up outbound."

Best for: top of funnel, opinion posts

The Pain-Point Hook
"[Describe the exact pain in their words]. Here's what's actually causing it:"

Examples:

"Your team is busy, your CRM is full, and nobody knows what is actually moving forward. Here's why:"

"You've got Apollo, Instantly, LinkedIn, Airtable and a CRM, but the process still feels manual. This is the gap:"

"Your outbound is not failing because of the copy. The follow-up layer is broken."

Best for: top + middle of funnel, attracts ICP

The Behind-the-Scenes Hook
"Most people don't talk about [thing]. We do it every day. Here's how it actually works:"

Examples:

"Most people don't talk about what happens between lead scraping and a booked call. We build that middle layer every day."

"Nobody talks about what actually breaks inside AI automation projects. It's usually not the AI."

"Most companies think automation means a workflow. The hard part is the operating logic around it."

Best for: middle of funnel, demonstrates depth

The Story Hook, for top of funnel
"[N] [time units] ago I [situation]. Today I [outcome]. What changed:"

Examples:

"A few years ago I was selling marketing services manually. Today I build systems that turn sales, content and CRM into one machine. The shift wasn't tools, it was architecture."

"I used to think AI automation was about saving time. Now I think it's about making small teams commercially dangerous."

Best for: top of funnel, attracts followers

Hooks We NEVER Write

"I'm excited to announce..."

"Just got off a call with [name drop]..."

Any hook that starts with "I" as the literal first word unless the result is strong enough

"5 ways to [generic verb]..." too vague, no specificity

"A lesson I learned about..." the lesson needs to BE the hook

"Stop [X]. Start [Y]." anything. fortune cookie, banned

The system that turns posts into pipeline
Every post hits one of three layers.

Mix the right ratio and your content drives leads.

Mix the wrong ratio and you build an audience that never converts.

Top of Funnel, TOFU - Attract
Goal: Get new eyeballs on your profile. Build the audience.

Post types:

Stories: how you got started, wins, losses

Shallow ideas / observations the ICP already agrees with

Personal life with a business angle

Hot takes on AI, automation, GTM, sales, agencies and operations

Behind-the-scenes of building the company

Goal of every TOFU post:

Get them to your PROFILE.

Frequency: 2 of every 7 posts, about 30%

NOT this layer's job:

Convert.

Don't try to sell here, you'll just look thirsty.

Middle of Funnel, MOFU - Demonstrate
Goal: Show you actually know what you're doing.

Demonstrate depth without giving away the entire service.

Post types:

Tactical breakdowns: here's HOW we do this thing

Frameworks YOU built

"Here's how we think about X" positioning posts

Industry observations that show you see the space differently

Objection handling: the things prospects worry about, addressed publicly

Breakdown of systems, workflows, tools and operating logic

Goal of every MOFU post:

Make the reader think "ok this person actually gets it" → click profile → eventually become a lead.

Frequency: 3 of every 7 posts, about 45%

This is the workhorse layer.

Most of your posts should be here.

Bottom of Funnel, BOFU - Convert
Goal: Turn readers into action takers.

DM you, book a call, download a lead magnet.

Post types:

Case studies with specific numbers

Lead magnet drops

"We've got capacity for X implementation slots this month" posts

Direct asks for the call

Breakdown of offers like the core offer, AI Ops System, GTM Infrastructure, Reporting Command Centre

Frequency: 2 of every 7 posts, about 30%

CTA on every BOFU post:

DM me [keyword]

Comment "[keyword]" and I'll send the doc

Link in comments to book

The lead magnet drop is the highest-leverage post type in the entire system.

People who comment for a free doc just told you they have the problem.

Now they're in your DMs.

Now you have a conversation.
